## What Is AI Engineer Singapore?

AI Engineer Singapore is the first Asia edition of AI Engineer, taking place May 15-17, 2026 at The Capitol Kempinski / Capitol Theatre in Singapore. The conference brings together engineers, founders, designers, operators, and technical leaders building with AI. Programming spans software, design, robotics + foundation models, and leadership, with speakers and teams from OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Cursor, Cloudflare, Vercel, Arize, LlamaIndex, AWS, Stripe, and others.

Unlike a generic AI conference, AI Engineer is designed around practical work: production stories, technical walkthroughs, lessons from teams building agents and AI tools, and candid discussion of what is working or breaking in the field.

## Track Goal

The Leadership Track is a practical decision forum for leaders moving from AI pilots to production outcomes. The strongest sessions should help attendees answer:

- Which AI workflows should we fund, scale, redesign, or kill?
- How should we staff AI work across central teams, product teams, business teams, and vendors?
- How do we govern agents and AI workflows in regulated or high-stakes environments?
- How do we measure reliability, ROI, adoption, eval quality, cost, risk, and production ownership?
- What should other leaders copy or avoid based on real implementation stories?

The intended promise: leaders leave with a practical view of how to choose, govern, staff, launch, and monitor AI workflows inside their organizations.

## Facilitation Goal

The 90-minute closing session should make the day meaningfully different from passive conference attendance. We want participants to synthesize what they learned, compare notes with peers, and leave with sharper insights from the people in the room.

The working format is to divide attendees into groups of roughly 20-30, segmented either by company type / stage, such as startup, enterprise, public sector, regulated industry, AI-native builder, or by problem statement, such as ROI, org design, governance, evals, production operations, and adoption.

Participants will identify one live AI leadership problem, discuss it with peers in a structured format, gather input from the group, and capture concrete next actions. Facilitators will keep the conversation practical, draw out useful examples, manage airtime, and help surface patterns for the final synthesis.

## Facilitator Ask

Before the session, facilitators will join a short briefing with Sherry to review the participant profile, prompts, group structure, and room logistics.

During the session, each facilitator will guide one group or segment, keep the discussion grounded in concrete examples and tradeoffs, and capture recurring insights, questions, and next actions.

After the session, facilitators will share quick notes with the AI Engineer team for synthesis.
